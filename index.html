📊 Key Finding: August Follows a Consistent Year-Over-Year Pattern
August Average Daily Rate dips are consistent across all years — 2023 ($215.88), 2024 ($215.22), and 2025 ($219.88). This reflects a structural pattern where leisure summer demand, while high in volume, supports a more competitive pricing environment than convention-heavy months like October. The Average Daily Rate is in the lowest tier of the year regardless of occupancy level, demonstrating that the two metrics do not always move in lockstep.
Answer — Question 1

Why Is August Average Daily Rate Lower Despite 70% Occupancy?

Structural Seasonal Pattern
August Average Daily Rate dips are consistent year-over-year across 2023, 2024, and 2025. This is not an anomaly; it is a recurring market dynamic. August represents a transition from peak summer leisure to fall convention season.
Leisure vs. Convention Mix
August is dominated by leisure travel, including family and theme park visitation. Hotels compete on rate to fill rooms. This sits alongside a diverse event mix that includes both Anaheim Convention Center citywides and self-contained events across the city.
Event Impact: 2024 vs. 2025
August 2024 had 25 events with 104,500 attendees but only 27,000 room nights. Those 25 events included 2 citywides and 23 non‑citywide events not using the Anaheim Convention Center. August 2025 had 19 events but 42,500 room nights and 64,200 attendees; those 19 events included 3 citywides and 16 non‑citywide events not using the Convention Center. Different event profiles — both ACC and non‑ACC — produce different rate pressures, and together they shape Anaheim’s overall demand, occupancy, and Average Daily Rate.

The market is not underperforming; it is behaving exactly as it has in prior years. October remains the highest Average Daily Rate month, driven by convention and meeting business.

📅 June 15 = Father's Day Sunday
Sundays structurally carry lower occupancy as weekend guests check out. June 15 fell on Father's Day, a holiday weekend, which sustained elevated pricing even as room counts dropped.
💰 Hotel Pricing Decision
Hotels independently set higher rates for the Father's Day weekend — this is a hotel-side pricing decision not directly controlled by Visit Anaheim. The Average Daily Rate of $225 on Sunday reflects weekend rate integrity, not a demand spike.
🎪 7 Major Events in June 2025
June 2025 featured 7 significant events including VidCon, supporting above-average demand across the month. Monthly Average Daily Rate of $248 (event average) and STR-reported $232.52 reflect a strong event-supported base rate environment.
📌 Bottom Line
High Average Daily Rate + lower occupancy on June 15 is a Sunday holiday pattern, not a market anomaly. Rate reflects weekend pricing integrity; occupancy reflects natural Sunday checkout dynamics.
